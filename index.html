The Museum of Lost Experiences, a crowd-sourced collection of artistic representations about grief, aims to be a platform for people to connect around losses big and small and process our experiences creatively in a community space.

Problem

Each of us encounters loss at some point in our lives. Those losses can be big, or small and affect us in different ways. While loss is universal, it affects each of us differently and it is common to feel alone in our experiences.

Inspiration

This project was initially inspired by the changes and losses we’ve all experienced due to the pandemic. Now more than ever many of us are dealing with difficult feelings, as we face a seismic shift in our daily lives due to Covid-19.

Whether our pain comes from a change in routine, the death of someone dear, or missing an opportunity; acknowledging that loss and expressing it can help us move through our feelings—experiencing and releasing them.

Through my capstone project, I wanted to develop one possible solution for dealing with the challenging feelings brought up by loss. I began the project researching related topics like grief, and different, and the use of creativity, nostalgia, and mindfulness as tools for handling difficult emotions.

Thoughts Toward a Solution

How do we deal with difficult emotions? There is no right way to process loss, but research supports using creativity as a tool for expression and mindfulness.

Art therapy is a recognized form of therapy that utilizes “active art-making, creative process, applied psychological theory, and human experience within a psychotherapeutic relationship.” But you don’t need to attend therapy to take advantage of the benefits of mindful creative practice.

Creativity comes in many forms, giving individuals the opportunity to find creative outlets that are enjoyable and meaningful to them. Through difficult times in my own life, I have used poetry, collage, and crafting for comfort, expression, and mindfulness practice.

Project Concept

The Museum of Lost Experiences aims to be a platform for people to connect around losses big and small and use creativity to process uncomfortable emotions. The project will give people an opportunity to process grief by creating a creative representation of a lost experience to submit to the collection where it will live alongside others’ experiences.

What we miss says a lot about what we care about and stepping into someone else’s experience is an intimate encounter with what is important to them. By encouraging viewers to engage with others’ stories of loss, the Museum of Lost Experiences helps reinforce that viewers are not alone in experiencing challenges in life.

Branding & Identity Design

The Coronavirus pandemic has caused a seismic shift in our daily lives and we are all grieving our loss of experiences. While loss is universal, it affects each of us differently and it is common to feel alone in our experiences. The Museum of Lost Experiences—a crowd-sourced collection of artistic representations about things we miss—aims to be a platform for people to connect around losses big and small and use creativity to process uncomfortable emotions.

Whether our pain comes from a change in routines, the death of someone close, or missing out on an opportunity; acknowledging that loss and expressing it can help us move through our feelings—experiencing and releasing them. The project will give people an opportunity to process grief by creating an artistic representation of their loss to submit it to the collection where it will live alongside others’ stories.

What we miss says a lot about what we care about and stepping into someone else’s experience is an intimate encounter with what is important to them. The Museum of Lost Experiences encourages viewers to engage with others’ stories of loss in order to reinforce that we are not alone in experiencing challenges in life. The project reimagines what a museum looks like by allowing submissions to be explored on the website, or checked out to view at home.
